Hello — pleased to meet you.

A designer chasing the unknown.

I'm Pathmesh Jayamurugan — a UX designer who came to interfaces through animation and brand design. I learn by making, by reading widely, and by sitting with problems for longer than feels comfortable.

An individual passionately driven to learn new things and continuously improve knowledge in design and beyond. I spend most of my time chasing the parts of a problem I don't yet understand — that's the work I find most rewarding.

I started in animation at Alagappa University, India, where I learned how to think in time, motion, and frame. I moved into graphic design — print, brand, layout — at Edward Language Academy and then freelance, working across visual hierarchy, typography, and visual systems.

What led me to UX was a question I couldn't answer with graphic design alone: why do well-designed-looking things still fail their users? The answer is research, evaluation, and the discipline of designing for someone, not at them. I'm now completing my MSc User Experience Design at the University of Brighton, working at the intersection of research, interaction design, and interface craft.

The projects on this site are my answer in progress. Each one tries to make the UX reasoning visible — not just the screens it produced.
